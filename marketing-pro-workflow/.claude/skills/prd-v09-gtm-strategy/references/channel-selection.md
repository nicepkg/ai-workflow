# Channel Selection Guide

**Purpose**: Framework for selecting marketing channels based on product type, audience, budget, and stage.

---

## Channel Deep Dive

### Channel 1: Email Marketing (Owned List)

**Best For**:
- Launching to existing users
- High engagement audience (newsletter, waitlist)
- Low/no budget

**Metrics**:
- Open rate: 20-30% (good)
- Click rate: 3-5% (good)
- Conversion: 1-3% of clicks ‚Üí signup

**Tactics**:

**Announcement Email** (Launch Day):
```
Subject: [Feature Name] is here üéâ
Preview: [One sentence benefit]

Body:
- Problem reminder (you asked for this)
- Solution reveal (here it is)
- Screenshot/GIF (show don't tell)
- CTA (try it now)

Send time: 10 AM local time (highest open rates)
```

**Drip Campaign** (Days 1-7):
- Day 1: Announcement (what's new)
- Day 3: Education (how to use it)
- Day 7: Social proof (others are using it)

**Segmentation**:
- Power users: "This will make you even faster"
- Inactive users: "Come back and see what's new"
- Free tier: "Unlock this with upgrade"

### Channel 2: Product Hunt

**Best For**:
- Tech products (SaaS, dev tools, productivity)
- B2B and prosumer
- Early adopter audience

**ROI Expectations**:
- Top 5 product: 300-1,000 signups
- Top 10 product: 100-300 signups
- Featured: 50-100 signups

**Launch Playbook**:

**Pre-Launch** (Week Before):
- [ ] Recruit 20-30 supporters (team, customers, friends)
- [ ] Prepare assets (tagline, screenshots, 60s demo video)
- [ ] Schedule for Tuesday-Thursday (avoid Monday/Friday, weekends)

**Launch Day**:
- [ ] Go live 12:01 AM PT (full day on homepage)
- [ ] Founder posts "Maker Comment" (personal story, why built, ask for feedback)
- [ ] Supporters upvote in first hour (algorithm boost)
- [ ] Respond to EVERY comment within 1 hour
- [ ] Share on Twitter with #ProductHunt tag

**Post-Launch**:
- [ ] Thank supporters
- [ ] Add "Featured on Product Hunt" badge to site
- [ ] Follow up with hunters who upvoted (nurture leads)

**Anti-Patterns**:
- ‚ùå Launching on weekends (lower traffic)
- ‚ùå Ignoring comments (looks abandoned)
- ‚ùå Over-promoting to email list (PH penalizes vote brigading)

### Channel 3: Content Marketing / SEO

**Best For**:
- Long sales cycle (B2B, enterprise)
- Educational products (complex, needs explaining)
- Competitive keywords (take market share)

**Timeline**: 3-6 months to see results (slow burn)

**ROI Expectations**:
- Good article: 500-5,000 views/month after 6 months
- Conversion: 1-3% of visitors ‚Üí signup
- Compounding: Traffic grows monthly

**Content Types**:

**How-To Guides**:
- "How to [achieve outcome]"
- Example: "How to build a sales dashboard in 10 minutes" (for BI tool)
- SEO: High search volume, moderate competition

**Comparison Pages**:
- "[Your Product] vs [Competitor]"
- Example: "Notion vs Confluence: Which is better for teams?"
- SEO: High intent, captures switching users

**Best Practices**:
- "[Topic] Best Practices" or "Ultimate Guide to [Topic]"
- Example: "Email Marketing Best Practices for SaaS"
- SEO: Moderate volume, authority building

**Strategy**:
- Publish 2-4 articles/month
- Target long-tail keywords (easier to rank)
- Link to product in context (not forced)
- Repurpose to social, email, newsletters

---

### Channel 4: Paid Search (Google Ads)

**Best For**:
- High intent keywords ("project management software", "accounting tool")
- Established category (people searching for solution)
- Short sales cycle

**ROI Expectations**:
- CPC: $1-50 (depends on competition)
- Conversion rate: 2-10% (landing page quality)
- CAC: $20-500 (industry dependent)

**Keyword Strategy**:

**Branded** (Defend):
- "YourProduct" (protect your brand)
- "YourProduct vs Competitor" (capture comparisons)

**Category** (Capture Demand):
- "project management software"
- "best email tool for teams"

**Problem** (Create Demand):
- "how to track time automatically"
- "reduce email overload"

**Competitor** (Steal Market Share):
- "Alternative to [Competitor]"
- "Cheaper than [Competitor]"

**Best Practices**:
- Start with branded + problem keywords (lower CPC)
- Build dedicated landing pages (not homepage)
- A/B test ad copy (benefit vs feature)
- Set max CPA (cost per acquisition) to stay profitable

---

### Channel 5: Paid Social (Facebook, LinkedIn, Twitter)

**Best For**:
- B2C products (visual, impulse)
- Broad awareness (introduce new category)
- Retargeting (nurture leads)

**Platform Selection**:

**Facebook/Instagram**:
- B2C, visual products
- Broad demographic targeting
- Low CPC ($0.50-2.00)

**LinkedIn**:
- B2B, professional
- Job title targeting (CTOs, Marketers)
- High CPC ($3-10)

**Twitter/X**:
- Tech, early adopters
- Interest + keyword targeting
- Medium CPC ($1-3)

**Ad Formats**:

**Awareness** (Top of Funnel):
- Video ad (30-60s demo)
- Carousel (show features)
- Goal: Brand awareness, video views

**Consideration** (Middle of Funnel):
- Lead ad (email capture)
- Link ad (to landing page)
- Goal: Signups, email list growth

**Conversion** (Bottom of Funnel):
- Retargeting (visited site, didn't sign up)
- Lookalike audiences (similar to customers)
- Goal: Trial signups, purchases

---

### Channel 6: Partnerships / Integrations

**Best For**:
- Complementary products (you + them = better together)
- Shared audience (no direct competition)
- Credibility boost

**Partnership Types**:

**Integration Partnership**:
- Build integration (e.g., Slack + Asana)
- Co-marketing (both promote integration)
- Example: "Now works with Slack!"

**Affiliate Partnership**:
- They promote, earn commission
- Works for: established brands with audience
- Example: Influencers promoting SaaS tools

**Co-Marketing**:
- Joint webinar, co-authored content
- Cross-promote to each other's lists
- Example: "Webinar: [Your Product] + [Their Product] for better workflows"

**Reseller/Channel**:
- They sell your product
- Works for: enterprise, complex sales
- Example: Consulting firms reselling your software

**ROI Expectations**:
- Integration: 5-20% of their users try your product
- Affiliate: 2-5% conversion from their audience
- Co-marketing: 10-30% email open rates, 50-200 signups

---

### Channel 7: Sales / Outbound

**Best For**:
- Enterprise (high ACV, complex sales)
- New category (need education)
- Niche market (small, identifiable)

**When NOT to Use**:
- Low ACV (< $1,000/year - too expensive)
- Self-serve product (sales overhead wasteful)
- Broad market (too many prospects, can't target)

**Outbound Playbook**:

**Step 1: Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)**
- Company size: 50-500 employees
- Industry: SaaS, tech
- Title: VP Product, CTO
- Pain: Struggling with [specific problem]

**Step 2: Build List** (100 prospects)
- LinkedIn Sales Navigator
- Industry databases (Crunchbase, AngelList)
- Verify emails (Hunter.io, Apollo)

**Step 3: Multi-Touch Sequence**
- Day 1: Email (personalized, short)
- Day 3: LinkedIn connection request
- Day 7: Follow-up email (share case study)
- Day 14: Phone call (if no response)

**Step 4: Qualify & Demo**
- Book 30-min demo
- Qualify (budget, authority, need, timeline)
- Send proposal if qualified

**Metrics**:
- Email open rate: 40-60%
- Response rate: 5-15%
- Demo conversion: 20-40%
- Close rate: 10-30% of demos

---

### Channel 8: Community Building

**Best For**:
- Niche audience (hobbyists, enthusiasts)
- Long-term play (build cult following)
- Developer tools, B2D (Business to Developer)

**Community Types**:

**Slack/Discord Community**:
- Direct access to users
- Real-time feedback
- Example: Indie Hackers, Y Combinator

**Forum (Discourse)**:
- Long-form discussions
- SEO benefit (indexed)
- Example: Developer forums

**Subreddit**:
- Leverage existing community
- Less control, more organic
- Example: r/SaaS, r/startups

**Strategy**:
- Provide value FIRST (not just promote)
- Answer questions, share expertise
- Occasional soft pitch (10% promotion, 90% value)
- Turn community into customers over time

**ROI**: Slow burn, hard to quantify. Best for: developer tools, B2D.

---

### Channel 9: PR / Press

**Best For**:
- Big launch (funding, major product)
- Consumer products (mass appeal)
- Credibility boost (appear in TechCrunch)

**When It Works**:
- Newsworthy angle (unique, first, biggest)
- Founder story (interesting background)
- Contrarian take (disagree with status quo)

**When It Doesn't**:
- "Me too" product (boring)
- No traction (journalists want proof)
- Local news (unless local business)

**PR Playbook**:

**Step 1: Build Journalist List**
- Research writers who cover your space
- Follow on Twitter, read their articles
- Note their interests, beats

**Step 2: Craft Pitch**
- Subject: Short, specific, newsworthy
- Body: 3 paragraphs max
  - Hook (why this matters NOW)
  - What you're doing (specific)
  - Proof (traction, customer stories)
- CTA: "Can I send more info?"

**Step 3: Timing**
- Tuesday-Thursday (best days)
- Morning (9-11 AM their timezone)
- Embargo (give exclusive to one, then broader)

**Step 4: Follow-Up**
- If no response in 3 days: one follow-up
- Don't spam (they'll blacklist you)

**ROI**: Varies wildly. TechCrunch feature: 500-5,000 signups. Local news: 10-50.

### Channel 10: Influencer Marketing

**Best For**:
- B2C, visual products
- Young demographic (Gen Z, Millennials)
- Products with "wow" factor

**Influencer Tiers**:

**Nano** (1K-10K followers):
- High engagement (5-10%)
- Affordable ($50-500/post)
- Niche audiences

**Micro** (10K-100K):
- Medium engagement (3-5%)
- Affordable ($500-5K/post)
- Targeted audiences

**Macro** (100K-1M):
- Lower engagement (1-3%)
- Expensive ($5K-50K/post)
- Broad awareness

**Mega** (1M+):
- Lowest engagement (<1%)
- Very expensive ($50K-500K+)
- Mass market

**Strategy**:
- Start with micro (better ROI)
- Authentic fit (use your product already?)
- Track with unique codes (know ROI)

## Channel Selection Framework

### Step 1: Know Your Customer Acquisition Cost (CAC) Budget

**Formula**:
```
CAC Budget = (Customer LTV √ó Target Margin) √∑ Payback Period
```

**Example**:
- LTV: $500 (customer lifetime value)
- Target Margin: 30%
- Payback Period: 12 months
- CAC Budget: ($500 √ó 0.3) √∑ 1 = $150/customer

**Channel Selection**:
- If CAC < $20: Organic (SEO, community, email)
- If CAC $20-100: Paid social, partnerships
- If CAC $100-500: Paid search, influencers
- If CAC > $500: Sales, enterprise

---

### Step 2: Match Channel to Product Type

**Clone** (Fast Follow):
- **Channels**: Paid search (competitor keywords), comparison SEO
- **Why**: Capture switching users

**Unbundle** (Niche):
- **Channels**: Community, content, partnerships
- **Why**: Target specific use case

**Undercut** (Price Leader):
- **Channels**: Paid search, comparison pages, affiliates
- **Why**: Lead with price

**Slice** (Wedge):
- **Channels**: Industry forums, LinkedIn (job title targeting), events
- **Why**: Hyper-targeted

**Wrapper** (Integration):
- **Channels**: Partner co-marketing, app marketplaces
- **Why**: Leverage ecosystem

**Innovation** (New Category):
- **Channels**: PR, content, influencers
- **Why**: Educate market

---

### Step 3: Match Channel to Stage

**Pre-Launch** (Validation):
- **Channels**: Community, email (waitlist), Product Hunt
- **Why**: Build anticipation, get feedback

**Launch** (0-100 customers):
- **Channels**: Product Hunt, email, personal outreach
- **Why**: Quick wins, manual work OK

**Growth** (100-1,000 customers):
- **Channels**: Paid search, paid social, content
- **Why**: Scalable, repeatable

**Scale** (1,000+ customers):
- **Channels**: All of the above + sales, PR, events
- **Why**: Multi-channel diversification

## Channel Testing Framework

### Week 1-2: Test 3 Channels

Pick 3 channels, allocate small budget to each:
- Channel A: $500
- Channel B: $500
- Channel C: $500

**Measure**:
- Signups per channel
- CAC per channel
- Quality (activation rate)

---

### Week 3-4: Double Down on Winner

- Kill lowest performer
- 2x budget on winner
- Keep testing second-best

**Example Results**:
- Paid search: 50 signups, $10 CAC ‚Üê Winner
- Paid social: 20 signups, $25 CAC ‚Üê Keep testing
- Influencers: 5 signups, $100 CAC ‚Üê Kill

**Week 3-4 Allocation**:
- Paid search: $1,000 (double down)
- Paid social: $500 (keep testing)
- Influencers: $0 (killed)

## Common Mistakes

### Mistake 1: Spreading Too Thin

**Problem**: Trying 10 channels with $100 each (not enough signal)

**Fix**: Focus on 2-3 channels with real budget

---

### Mistake 2: Giving Up Too Early

**Problem**: "We tried paid search for 1 week, didn't work"

**Fix**: Run for 4-6 weeks, iterate (landing page, ad copy, keywords)

---

### Mistake 3: Ignoring Owned Channels

**Problem**: Spending $10K on ads, ignoring email list

**Fix**: Always start with owned (email, in-app), then paid

---

### Mistake 4: No Attribution

**Problem**: Don't know which channel drove signups

**Fix**: Use UTM codes, track source in database
