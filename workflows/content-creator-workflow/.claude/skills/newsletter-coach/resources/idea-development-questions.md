# Idea Development Questions

Use these questions in Phase 1 to help extract and develop ideas from the writer's experience.

---

## 1. Conventional Wisdom

* What do most people / experts say about this topic?
* What are most people doing or saying?
* What are most people not willing to say or do?
* Who are the experts on this topic?
* Who solved this problem in other industries?
* Who solved this problem in my industry?
* Who's doing this better?
* What does the science say?
* How about history?

---

## 2. Personal Knowledge & Experience

* What do I know about this topic?
* What do I know about this topic that 80% of my readers don't know?
* What do I know that my reader may know, but they just haven't thought to say it?
* What don't I know about it?
* What do I want to know about it?
* What successes have I had in my work on it?
* What mistakes have I made?
* What excites me about it?
* What scares me?
* What stories can I tell from my life?

---

## 3. Insight & Framing

* What's the best advice I know about this topic?
* What's the worst advice I know about this topic?
* How can I reframe it?
* What are my assumptions about it?
* What's the hard part?
* What's the core issue?
* Why do we do that?
* Has it always been this way?
* What does that word mean anyway?
* What's controversial about this?
* What's my spin on that news or trend?
* What lesson does this reinforce?
* How could it challenge your current beliefs?

---

## 4. Structure & Breakdown

* What are the benefits?
* What are the roadblocks?
* What are the pieces I could divide it into?
* What are the parts? Can I explore each?
* What are the components, contents, building blocks of this [idea, knowledge, design, etc..]?
* What stories do I have for each part?
* What are some facts?
* What stories about it come to mind?
* What images come to mind?
* What metaphors and analogies?

---

## 5. Problem & Opportunity

* Who is involved with the problem?
* What frustrates me? Why?
* What's the hard part?
* What are potential reasons not to do this, i.e. counterpoints?
* What are areas of risk and how might we mitigate?
* What else could you add to make it more compelling?
* What else is interesting about this?
* What new insights does it reveal?
* What benefit could be gained from making a connection?

---

## 6. Evidence & Support

* What evidence, data, and examples am I pulling from?
* What kind of proof or story would enhance it?
* What does success look like?
* What's the upside, downside, and trade-offs?
* Why is this likely to work and be a good investment?
* What kind of connection would be transformative?
* What would a connection disprove?
* What are their obvious differences?
* How can you link up those differences?
* How does this real life experience prove or explain this [idea, knowledge, design, etc..]?

---

## 7. Audience & Purpose

* Who is this for? What is this for?
* Why would your target audience be excited to do the thing you want them to do?
* Who would feel threatened by this and why?
* Who would be the ideal recipient and why?
* What do I say to skeptics?
* If your reader walks away remembering only one thing, what would it be?

---

## 8. Strategy & Execution

* What small experiments might I try?
* How can we test this in the lowest-overhead way possible?
* What assets, levers, and constraints are we working with?
* Who do we need buy-in from in order to move forward?
* How does this stack rank against other ideas for driving the business?
* If we decided to move forward today, what would you need to bring this to life?
* Can we do this ourselves, or do we need to tap into other teams?
* What is our unfair advantage for doing this?
* Is the juice worth the squeeze?

---

## 9. Connection & Synthesis

* What other topics does this one remind me of?
* What do they share in common?
* What is the connection between these two things [experiences, ideas, knowledge, designs, etc?]
* Describe your various [ideas, experiences, etc.]. What individual words do they share in common?
* What is this an example of?
* How would you sell the idea if your life depended on it?

---

## 10. Relevance & Timing

* Why is it important right now?
* Why is this likely to work and be a good investment?
